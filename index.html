Tourist gallery

WATERFALL 1

"El Bebedero"

Description: This first waterfall appears as an elegant curtain of water cascading down a black, mossy rock face. The drop isn't steep, but its beauty lies in the texture of the rock and the jungle setting. The reflection of the water creates a serene atmosphere, ideal for beginning the adventure with tranquility and connection with nature..

WATERFALL 2

“Cascada Seca” (El Bejuco)

Description: Although its flow is minimal, this waterfall has a history: it feeds the local community. Its greatest significance is cultural and environmental, as it demonstrates how the environment impacts the lives of those who live nearby. The guide often highlights its function as a water source and the value of conserving the surrounding ecosystems.

WATERFALL 3

“El Arcoíris”

Description: This waterfall stands out for its thin veil of water that, when the light hits it in a certain way, forms beautiful rainbows over the rocky bottom. People can get close and feel the spray on their skin; the reflection of the natural tones of the forest and the water creates a magical atmosphere.

WATERFALL 4

“La Escalada”

Description: Here begins the most exciting part: a tall, fast-flowing waterfall that requires climbing with a rope provided by the guides. It is estimated to reach a height of about 40 meters. Energy and adrenaline increase as you overcome this section, while the water sprays hikers who strive to conquer this stage of the trail.

WATERFALL 5

“La Caverna”

Description: This waterfall is distinguished by its cascading waterfall, which falls directly into a cave or rocky cavity. Visitors can enter behind the veil of water and observe the landscape from within, creating a unique and entertaining experience. The sound reverberates within the cavern, enveloping visitors in a magical and refreshing atmosphere. It is one of the most photographed spots on the route.

WATERFALL 6

“El Tobogán”

Description: It gets its name because the water gently cascades down a sloping rock, forming a natural slide. Many hikers take advantage of the opportunity to slide across the wet surface, turning it into an interactive, adrenaline-filled waterfall. A clear, deep pool forms at its foot, ideal for swimming and relaxing.

WATERFALL 7

“La Corona”

Description: The last and most impressive waterfall on the route, called "The Crown" because it symbolizes the culmination of the challenge. Its drop is high, wide, and powerful, surrounded by green walls and abundant vegetation. Guides often stop here to celebrate the feat of completing all seven waterfalls. It's the perfect place to rest, take photos, and enjoy the scenery before returning.

7 Waterfall Tours

El Salvador, a moderately challenging, guided adventure of approximately 5 km that follows a waterfall circuit amidst lush vegetation, includes river crossings and climbing some of the falls. It's a popular activity for enjoying nature, swimming in pools, and experiencing the unique atmosphere of the Flower Route.

Local gastronomy

"(The 7 Waterfalls) depends on the specific location, as there is no single site with that name. However, if you're referring to the 7 Cascadas (7 Waterfalls) of Juayúa, El Salvador, you can expect to try typical Salvadoran dishes in town, such as pupusas and dishes based on fresh regional produce.
